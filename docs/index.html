Abstract

Robotic learning has emerged as a promising tool for taming the complexity and diversity of open-world robotics problems. Methods based on high-capacity models, such as deep networks, hold the promise of providing effective generalization to a wide range of open-world environments. However, these same methods typically require large amounts of diverse training data to generalize effectively. In contrast, most robotic learning experiments are small-scale, single-domain, and single-robot. This leads to a frequent tension in robotic learning: how can we learn generalizable robotic controllers without having to collect impractically large amounts of data for each separate experiment?

To address this question we propose RoboNet, an open database for sharing robotic experience, which provides an initial pool of 13 million video frames, from 6 different robot manipulators, that can be used to learn generalizable models. In our experiments, we study how video prediction models trained on the full dataset and its subsets can enable vision-based control of robotic manipulators that generalizes not just to new objects, new tasks, and new scenes, but also to new viewpoints, grippers, and entirely new robots. These models predict future observations conditioned on the current observation and candidate actions, enabling learned model-based control for robotic manipulation via the visual foresight algorithm. We finally study how performance on any single robot or domain can be further improved via finetuning, with a small amount of in-domain data.

RoboNet: By the Numbers

13M Frames

RoboNet contains over 13 million video frames of robot-object interaction.

6 Robots

We've collected data over 6 robots and 7 grippers! RoboNet can scale to new data from any robot.

4 Institutions

Our dataset was collected thanks to the combined effort of 4 different academic and industry institutions.
